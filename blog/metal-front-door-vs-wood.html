Metal Front Door vs Wood Door: Which is Better for Your Home?

April 9, 2026 8 min read By Legendary Metal Works

Your front door is the first thing guests see and the last line of defense between your family and the outside world. When it is time to replace or upgrade your entry door, the choice usually comes down to two options: metal or wood.

Both have passionate advocates, and both have real strengths. But which one is actually better for your home? In this comprehensive guide, we compare metal front doors (iron and steel) against wood doors across every factor that matters: security, durability, energy efficiency, maintenance, cost, curb appeal, weather resistance, and customization.

Security: Metal Doors Win Decisively

When it comes to protecting your home, metal front doors are in a completely different league than wood.

A steel entry door is virtually impossible to kick in. The material resists forced entry, drilling, and cutting far better than any wood species. Iron doors offer similar protection with even greater thickness and mass. Most metal entry doors also feature reinforced lock pockets, multi-point locking systems, and heavy-duty hinges that make break-ins extraordinarily difficult.

Wood doors, even solid hardwood ones, are inherently more vulnerable. They can be forced open with enough impact, and the wood around the lock and hinge areas is a weak point that intruders can exploit. While reinforcement kits and deadbolts improve wood door security, they still cannot match the structural integrity of a metal door.

Verdict: If home security is a priority, a metal front door is the clear choice. This is one of the most compelling steel entry door benefits that homeowners consistently cite.

Durability and Lifespan: Metal Outlasts Wood by Decades

A quality iron door or steel door will last 50 to 100 years with proper finishing. Metal does not warp, crack, shrink, swell, or rot. It is impervious to termites and other wood-boring pests. The only real threat to a metal door's longevity is corrosion, and modern powder-coating and galvanization techniques have made rust a manageable concern rather than an inevitability.

Wood doors, by contrast, are organic materials that are in a constant battle with the elements. Even premium hardwoods like mahogany and white oak will eventually succumb to moisture damage, UV degradation, and the natural expansion and contraction cycles caused by temperature and humidity changes. A well-maintained wood front door typically lasts 20 to 40 years — respectable, but significantly less than metal.

In a coastal climate like Ventura, where moisture and salt air are ever-present, the durability gap between metal and wood widens further. Wood doors in coastal environments often show signs of wear within just a few years if maintenance lapses.

Verdict: Metal doors offer two to three times the lifespan of wood doors, making them the more durable long-term investment.

Energy Efficiency: Closer Than You Might Think

Energy efficiency is one area where the comparison is more nuanced than many people expect.

Steel doors with polyurethane foam cores are among the most energy-efficient entry doors available. The insulated core provides excellent thermal resistance, and steel's tight tolerances create reliable weathertight seals. Many insulated steel doors achieve R-values of 10 or higher.

Iron doors with glass panels are a different story. The glass inserts, while beautiful, can reduce insulation performance. However, modern iron doors use double-paned, low-E tempered glass and thermal breaks within the frame to minimize heat transfer. The result is a door that balances aesthetics with reasonable energy performance.

Solid wood doors are natural insulators. Wood does not conduct heat the way metal does, which gives it an inherent advantage in raw thermal resistance. However, wood doors are susceptible to gaps forming over time as the material expands and contracts, which can undermine their insulating performance.

Verdict: Insulated steel doors are the most energy-efficient option. Solid wood performs well when new but can lose efficiency over time. Iron doors with glass panels trade some insulation for dramatic visual impact.

Maintenance: Metal Saves You Time and Money

Maintenance is where the metal front door vs wood comparison becomes especially one-sided.

A powder-coated metal door requires almost no routine maintenance. An occasional wipe-down with a damp cloth is typically all it needs. The finish resists fading, chipping, and peeling for years. If the finish is ever damaged, a touch-up is straightforward and inexpensive.

A wood front door demands significantly more attention. To maintain its appearance and structural integrity, you will need to:

  • Refinish or restain the door every 1 to 3 years
  • Sand and repaint if the finish cracks or peels
  • Inspect and treat for insect damage regularly
  • Check for warping, swelling, and seal gaps seasonally
  • Address any moisture intrusion immediately to prevent rot

Over the life of the door, maintenance costs for wood can add up to hundreds or even thousands of dollars. More importantly, it requires consistent attention — skip a year or two of refinishing, and you can end up with costly damage.

Verdict: Metal doors require a fraction of the maintenance time and cost. For busy homeowners, this alone can be a deciding factor.

Cost Comparison: Upfront vs Lifetime

Cost is often the first factor homeowners consider, but it is important to look at both the upfront price and the total cost of ownership.

Upfront Costs

  • Basic steel entry door: $500 - $2,000 (pre-fabricated)
  • Mid-range wood door: $1,000 - $3,000
  • Custom steel door: $2,000 - $5,000
  • Premium hardwood door: $2,500 - $6,000
  • Custom iron door with glass: $3,000 - $10,000+

Lifetime Cost Considerations

When you factor in maintenance and replacement, the picture shifts. A wood door that costs $2,500 upfront may require $200 to $400 in refinishing every two years, adding $2,000 to $4,000 over 20 years — plus the cost of eventual replacement. A custom metal door at $4,000 with minimal maintenance and a 50+ year lifespan often works out to a lower cost per year of service.

Verdict: Wood doors can be less expensive upfront, but metal doors typically offer better value over their full lifespan. A custom metal front door is an investment that pays for itself over time.

Curb Appeal and Resale Value

Your front door has an outsized impact on your home's curb appeal. According to industry data, a quality front door replacement is one of the highest-ROI exterior improvements you can make, with some projects recouping 50 to 90 percent of their cost at resale.

Metal doors, particularly custom iron doors with decorative glass panels, create a dramatic and luxurious first impression. They signal to buyers and visitors alike that the home has been upgraded with quality materials. A statement iron door can transform the entire look of a home's facade.

Wood doors offer their own kind of appeal — warm, organic, and classic. A beautifully finished wood door on the right home (particularly Craftsman, colonial, or rustic styles) can be stunning. However, that appeal is contingent on the door being in excellent condition. A weathered, peeling wood door hurts curb appeal rather than helping it.

Real estate agents consistently report that homes with custom metal entry doors attract more buyer attention and can command higher asking prices, particularly in upscale and coastal markets like Ventura County.

Verdict: Both materials can deliver excellent curb appeal when well-executed. Metal doors have the edge in longevity of appearance — they look as impressive in year 20 as they did on installation day.

Weather Resistance

Weather resistance is a critical factor, especially in Southern California where homes face intense sun, occasional heavy rain, and — along the coast — persistent salt air.

Metal doors handle sun exposure without warping, cracking, or fading (when properly powder-coated). They are unaffected by humidity changes and will not swell or stick in damp weather. In coastal areas like Ventura, a quality powder-coated steel or iron door with marine-grade finishing will stand up to salt air for years between maintenance cycles.

Wood doors are far more sensitive to weather. Direct sun causes UV damage and finish deterioration. Rain and humidity cause swelling, which can make the door stick or create gaps. Repeated wet-dry cycles lead to cracking and checking. South-facing and west-facing wood doors take the worst beating and require the most frequent refinishing.

Verdict: Metal doors are significantly more weather-resistant, making them the smarter choice for exposed or coastal installations.

Customization Options

If you want a front door that is truly one of a kind, both materials offer customization — but in different ways.

A custom metal front door can be fabricated in virtually any size, shape, and configuration. Single doors, double doors, doors with sidelights and transoms, arched tops, and pivot doors are all possible. Iron doors can incorporate hand-forged scrollwork, custom patterns, monograms, and decorative glass inserts in clear, frosted, textured, or stained varieties. The design possibilities are genuinely limitless.

Wood doors offer customization through carving, panel configurations, wood species selection, stain colors, and hardware choices. Wood can be combined with glass inserts as well, though the structural limitations of wood mean that very large glass panels require additional reinforcement.

At Legendary Metal Works, we specialize in fully custom metal doors designed from scratch for each client. Every door we build is unique — tailored to the homeowner's architectural style, personal taste, and functional requirements.

Verdict: Both materials offer strong customization. Metal doors have an edge in structural flexibility (larger sizes, more glass, unique shapes), while wood doors excel in natural texture and warmth.

Side-by-Side Summary

  • Security: Metal wins
  • Durability: Metal wins (50-100 years vs 20-40 years)
  • Energy efficiency: Insulated steel wins; solid wood is competitive
  • Maintenance: Metal wins decisively
  • Upfront cost: Wood can be less expensive
  • Lifetime value: Metal wins
  • Curb appeal: Both strong; metal has longer-lasting appeal
  • Weather resistance: Metal wins
  • Customization: Both excellent; metal offers more structural flexibility

Which Door Is Right for You?

If you value security, durability, low maintenance, and long-term value, a metal front door is the better choice for most homes. This is especially true in coastal and sun-exposed environments like Ventura County, where weather takes a heavy toll on wood.

A wood door may be the right call if you have a specific architectural style that demands natural wood (such as a historic Craftsman home), you are on a tight upfront budget, or you genuinely enjoy the ritual of maintaining and refinishing your door.

For many homeowners, the ideal solution is a custom iron or steel door that captures the warmth and character they love about wood — through design details, glass selections, and finish colors — while delivering the strength, security, and longevity that only metal can provide.
