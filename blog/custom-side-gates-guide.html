Custom Side Gates: The Complete Guide to Styles, Materials & Installation

April 9, 2026 10 min read By Legendary Metal Works

Your driveway gate gets all the attention, but the custom side gate is the unsung hero of property security. Side gates protect vulnerable access points along the sides of your home, keep children and pets safely contained, and add a polished finishing touch to your property's perimeter. Yet many homeowners overlook them entirely or settle for a flimsy off-the-shelf option that warps, sags, or rusts within a few years.

In this comprehensive guide, we cover everything you need to know about metal side gates — from styles and materials to hardware, building codes, costs, and installation — so you can make the right investment for your Ventura County home.

Why You Need a Quality Side Gate

A side gate does more than just fill a gap in your fence line. Here are the top reasons Ventura County homeowners invest in a custom side gate:

  • Security: Side yards are one of the most common entry points for burglars. A sturdy metal side gate with a quality lock eliminates easy access to your backyard, windows, and sliding doors.
  • Privacy: A well-designed pedestrian gate prevents strangers, solicitors, and delivery drivers from wandering into private areas of your property.
  • Child and pet safety: If you have kids or dogs, a self-closing side gate ensures no one accidentally leaves the yard open. This is especially critical for homes with pools.
  • Curb appeal: A custom side gate that matches your front fence or driveway gate creates a cohesive, high-end look that elevates your entire property.
  • Property value: Buyers notice when a home is fully secured and well-maintained. A quality side gate signals attention to detail and adds real resale value.

Types of Custom Side Gates

Not all side gates are created equal. The right style depends on your priorities — whether that is maximum security, privacy, aesthetics, or a combination of all three.

Full Metal Side Gates

Built entirely from iron, steel, or aluminum, full metal side gates are the gold standard for durability and security. They can be fabricated with an open picket design for visibility or with solid sheet metal panels for complete privacy. At Legendary Metal Works, full metal gates are our specialty, and we custom-build each one to fit your exact opening.

Wood and Metal Combo Gates

These gates feature a welded metal frame with wood plank infill. The steel frame provides structural integrity and resistance to sagging, while the wood delivers a warm, natural look and full privacy. This style is extremely popular in Ventura County neighborhoods where homeowners want the strength of metal without a purely industrial appearance.

Privacy Panel Gates

Privacy panel side gates use solid metal sheets, perforated metal, or laser-cut decorative panels within a metal frame. They block the view into your side yard completely while allowing airflow. Perforated and laser-cut options add an artistic element that sets your gate apart from the rest.

Ornamental Iron Gates

If curb appeal is your top priority, an ornamental iron side gate with scrollwork, finials, or custom motifs makes a statement. These gates work beautifully on Spanish-style, Mediterranean, and traditional homes — all of which are common throughout Ventura, Oxnard, Camarillo, and Thousand Oaks.

Choosing the Right Material for Your Side Gate

The material you choose affects durability, maintenance, weight, and cost. Here is how the most common options compare:

Wrought Iron

Wrought iron is the classic choice for custom gates. It is incredibly strong, takes ornamental detail well, and can last a lifetime with proper finishing. The trade-off is that iron requires periodic maintenance — touch-up paint and rust prevention — especially in coastal areas like Ventura County, where salt air accelerates corrosion.

Mild Steel

Steel is the most popular material for custom-fabricated side gates. It is strong, cost-effective, and easy to weld into virtually any design. Like iron, it needs a quality primer and paint or powder-coat finish to resist rust. Legendary Metal Works uses professional-grade powder coating on all our steel gates to ensure long-lasting protection against the elements.

Aluminum

Aluminum is naturally rust-resistant, making it an attractive option for properties near the coast. It is lighter than steel, which means less stress on hinges and posts. However, aluminum is not as strong as steel or iron, so it is better suited for standard pedestrian gates rather than heavy-duty security applications.

Wood and Metal Combination

A steel frame paired with redwood, cedar, or composite wood planks gives you the best of both worlds. The metal frame eliminates the sagging and warping issues that plague all-wood gates, while the wood provides privacy and a softer aesthetic. We recommend using naturally rot-resistant species like redwood or cedar, which hold up well in the Ventura County climate.

Sizing and Measurements: Getting It Right

Accurate measurements are critical. A side gate that is too small leaves gaps; one that is too large will not fit. Here is what you need to know:

  • Width: Most residential side yard openings range from 36 to 48 inches. Measure the opening at the top, middle, and bottom — walls and fences are rarely perfectly parallel.
  • Height: Side gates typically match the height of the adjacent fence, usually 5 to 6 feet. If security is a top priority, consider going up to 7 feet or adding decorative spear tops that discourage climbing.
  • Ground clearance: Leave 1 to 2 inches of clearance at the bottom for the gate to swing freely over uneven ground or concrete. For sloped side yards, the gate may need to be custom-tapered to follow the grade.
  • Post spacing: If new posts are needed, they should be set in concrete footings at least 18 to 24 inches deep for stability.

When you work with Legendary Metal Works, we handle all measurements on-site during your free consultation. We account for slopes, uneven walls, and existing structures so your gate fits perfectly the first time.

Hardware That Makes a Difference

The right hardware turns a good side gate into a great one. Do not overlook these components:

Self-Closing Hinges

Self-closing hinges use a spring mechanism to pull the gate shut automatically after every use. This is essential for pet owners and families with children, and it is actually required by California building code for any gate that provides access to a pool area.

Magnetic Latches

A magnetic latch ensures the gate closes securely and stays shut, even in windy conditions — something Ventura County homeowners know all too well. They are quiet, reliable, and pair well with self-closing hinges.

Deadbolt Locks and Keyed Latches

For security, a keyed deadbolt or keyed latch prevents unauthorized entry. We recommend a lock that can be operated from both sides for convenience, with a keyed exterior and thumb-turn interior.

Keypad and Smart Lock Entry

Keypad entry systems let you provide access codes to family members, dog walkers, landscapers, and housekeepers without handing out physical keys. Smart locks take it a step further with remote access via smartphone, temporary codes, and activity logs.

Heavy-Duty Hinges

Standard hinges are not built for the weight of a custom metal gate. We use heavy-duty, ball-bearing hinges rated for the specific weight of your gate. Properly specified hinges mean smooth operation for decades, not months.

Matching Your Side Gate to Your Driveway Gate

One of the most common design requests we receive at Legendary Metal Works is to build a side gate that complements an existing or new driveway gate. A cohesive look ties your entire property together and creates a professional, intentional aesthetic.

Here is how to achieve a unified design:

  • Match the material and finish: If your driveway gate is powder-coated black steel, your side gate should be the same.
  • Echo design elements: Pick up key details like picket style, scroll patterns, or horizontal rail spacing from your driveway gate and incorporate them into the side gate.
  • Scale appropriately: A side gate is smaller than a driveway gate, so ornamental details should be proportionally scaled down. A direct copy at a smaller size can look awkward.
  • Coordinate hardware finishes: Black hinges and latches with a black gate, bronze hardware with a bronze-finished gate — small details matter.

When we fabricate both your driveway gate and side gate, we design them as a matched set from the start. If you already have a driveway gate from another fabricator, we can study its design and create a complementary side gate that looks like it was always part of the plan.

Building Code Considerations in Ventura County

Before installing a side gate, it is important to understand the local codes that may apply:

  • Pool barrier requirements: California Building Code (CBC) requires that all gates providing access to a pool or spa area must be self-closing, self-latching, and open outward (away from the pool). The latch must be at least 54 inches above the ground if on the pool side.
  • Height restrictions: In most Ventura County residential zones, side yard fences and gates can be up to 6 feet tall. Front yard or street-facing side gates may be limited to 3.5 to 4 feet depending on the setback and specific city codes in Ventura, Oxnard, Camarillo, or other local jurisdictions.
  • Permits: Most standard side gate replacements do not require a permit, but new installations that involve new posts, footings, or changes to the property line fence may. Check with your local building department or ask us — we navigate Ventura County permit requirements regularly.
  • HOA rules: Many communities in Ventura County have HOA guidelines dictating gate materials, colors, and styles. Review your CC&Rs before finalizing a design.

How Much Does a Custom Side Gate Cost?

Custom side gate pricing depends on size, material, design complexity, and hardware. Here are realistic ranges for professionally fabricated and installed side gates in Ventura County:

  • Basic steel pedestrian gate (simple picket or flat panel): $1,500 - $2,500
  • Steel frame with wood infill: $2,000 - $3,500
  • Ornamental iron gate with decorative details: $2,500 - $4,500
  • Privacy panel gate (solid or perforated metal): $2,500 - $4,000
  • High-end custom gate with smart lock, premium finish, and ornamental design: $4,500 - $8,000+

These prices include fabrication, finish, hardware, and professional installation. Factors that push costs toward the higher end include wider or taller openings, complex designs, premium hardware (keypad or smart lock), new post and footing installation, and expedited timelines.

Keep in mind that a custom metal side gate is a long-term investment. Unlike a pre-made gate from a home improvement store that may last 3 to 5 years, a professionally fabricated gate from Legendary Metal Works is built to last 20 years or more.

Maintenance Tips for Your Side Gate

A quality side gate requires minimal maintenance, but a little attention goes a long way:

  • Inspect the finish annually: Look for chips, scratches, or early signs of rust. Touch up any damage with matching paint or a rust-inhibiting primer before it spreads.
  • Lubricate hinges and latches: Apply a silicone-based lubricant to hinges, springs, and latch mechanisms every 6 months. This keeps the gate swinging smoothly and prevents squeaking.
  • Clean the gate: Wash your gate with mild soap and water once or twice a year to remove dirt, pollen, and salt residue — especially important for properties in coastal Ventura.
  • Check self-closing function: If your gate has self-closing hinges, test them periodically to ensure the gate latches fully on its own. Adjust spring tension as needed.
  • Inspect wood components: If you have a wood-and-metal combo gate, check the wood for signs of rot, warping, or fading. Reseal or restain as needed every 2 to 3 years.
  • Address drainage: Make sure water is not pooling at the base of the gate or posts. Standing water accelerates rust on metal and rot on wood.

How to Choose the Right Side Gate for Your Property

With so many options, here is a simple framework to narrow down your decision:

  1. Define your primary goal: Is it security, privacy, child/pet safety, aesthetics, or all of the above? This determines your gate style and height.
  2. Assess your environment: Coastal properties in Ventura need rust-resistant finishes or aluminum. Inland properties in Camarillo or Thousand Oaks have more material flexibility.
  3. Match your home's style: A modern home looks best with clean-line flat panel or horizontal slat gates. A Spanish-style home pairs beautifully with ornamental iron or arched designs.
  4. Set your budget: Know your range before you start shopping. A quality custom gate starts around $1,500 and scales with size and complexity.
  5. Choose your hardware: Decide on self-closing hinges, lock type, and whether you want smart access before fabrication begins — some hardware requires specific frame preparations.
  6. Work with a specialist: A dedicated metal fabrication shop like Legendary Metal Works will deliver a far superior product compared to a general contractor or handyman. We weld, grind, finish, and install every gate ourselves.
