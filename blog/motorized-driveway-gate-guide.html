The Complete Guide to Motorized Driveway Gates: Types, Costs & What to Expect

March 15, 2024 8 min read By Legendary Metal Works

A motorized driveway gate is one of the most impactful upgrades you can make to your property. It adds security, convenience, privacy, and serious curb appeal. But with so many options available, choosing the right gate can feel overwhelming.

In this guide, we break down everything you need to know — from gate types and motor systems to realistic costs and installation timelines — so you can make a confident, informed decision.

Swing Gates vs. Sliding Gates: Which is Right for You?

The two main types of motorized driveway gates are swing gates and sliding gates. Your choice depends on your driveway layout, available space, and aesthetic preferences.

Swing Gates

Swing gates open inward or outward like a door. They're the most popular choice for residential properties and come in single or double configurations.

  • Best for: Wide driveways with ample clearance space
  • Pros: Classic look, works on flat or slight inclines, wide design options
  • Cons: Requires clearance space for the gate to swing open, not ideal for steep driveways

Sliding Gates

Sliding gates move horizontally along a track or rail. They're ideal when space is limited or the driveway has a steep grade.

  • Best for: Limited space, steep driveways, commercial properties
  • Pros: No swing clearance needed, works on steep grades, can span very wide openings
  • Cons: Requires lateral wall/fence space for the gate to slide into, track needs periodic cleaning

Choosing the Right Motor System

The motor is the heart of your automatic gate. A quality motor system means reliable operation, quiet performance, and years of trouble-free use.

Key factors when choosing a motor:

  • Gate weight: Heavier gates (iron, steel) require more powerful motors
  • Duty cycle: How often will the gate open per day? Residential vs. commercial needs differ
  • Power source: Hardwired (most reliable), solar-powered (remote properties), or battery backup
  • Safety features: Auto-reverse sensors, obstacle detection, manual release for power outages

We typically recommend LiftMaster and Viking motor systems for their reliability, warranty support, and smart-home integration capabilities.

Access Control Options

Modern motorized gates offer multiple ways to control access:

  • Remote controls: Standard RF remotes for you and family members
  • Keypads: PIN-code entry for visitors, delivery drivers, and service providers
  • Smartphone apps: Open your gate from anywhere using your phone
  • Intercoms: Video or audio intercom systems to screen visitors
  • Vehicle sensors: Automatic opening when your car approaches

What Does a Motorized Driveway Gate Cost?

Costs vary based on size, material, design complexity, and motor system. Here's a general breakdown:

  • Basic single swing gate (steel, simple design): $3,000 - $6,000 installed
  • Double swing gate (ornamental iron): $6,000 - $15,000 installed
  • Sliding gate (steel/iron): $5,000 - $12,000 installed
  • High-end custom estate gate: $15,000 - $40,000+ installed

These ranges include the gate, motor system, basic access control (remote + keypad), and professional installation. Complex designs, premium materials, and advanced access control systems will be on the higher end.

Maintenance Tips

A well-maintained motorized gate can last 20+ years. Here are our top tips:

  • Lubricate hinges and moving parts every 3-6 months
  • Keep the track clean (for sliding gates)
  • Test safety sensors monthly
  • Inspect for rust and touch up paint as needed
  • Schedule annual professional maintenance
