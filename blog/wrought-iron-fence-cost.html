How Much Does a Wrought Iron Fence Cost in 2026? Complete Pricing Guide

June 10, 2024 (Updated 2026) 10 min read By Legendary Metal Works

If you are considering a wrought iron fence for your property, one of the first questions on your mind is probably: how much does a wrought iron fence cost? It is a fair question, and the answer depends on several factors including the length of the fence, the design complexity, your location, and the installer you choose.

At Legendary Metal Works Inc., we have been fabricating and installing custom wrought iron fences for homeowners and businesses throughout Ventura County for years. In this comprehensive guide, we break down everything you need to know about iron fence prices, what affects the cost, and how wrought iron compares to other fencing materials.

Wrought Iron Fence Cost Per Linear Foot

The most common way to price a wrought iron fence is by the linear foot. Here is what you can expect to pay in 2026:

  • Basic wrought iron fence: $25 - $45 per linear foot (materials only)
  • Mid-range ornamental iron fence: $35 - $65 per linear foot (materials only)
  • High-end custom wrought iron fence: $60 - $150+ per linear foot (materials only)

When you factor in professional installation, you are looking at a total metal fence cost per foot of roughly $40 to $200+, depending on the complexity of the design and site conditions.

For a typical residential property in Ventura County that needs 150 to 200 linear feet of fencing, that puts total project costs in the range of $6,000 to $30,000+ for a fully installed wrought iron fence.

Factors That Affect Wrought Iron Fence Cost

No two fencing projects are exactly alike. Here are the key variables that influence your final iron fence price:

1. Fence Height

Standard residential wrought iron fences are typically 4 to 6 feet tall. Taller fences require more material and labor, which increases cost. A 6-foot fence can cost 30-50% more than a 4-foot fence of the same design.

2. Design Complexity

A simple flat-top picket design is the most affordable option. As you add decorative elements like finials, scrollwork, spear tops, rings, or custom patterns, the cost increases. Fully custom designs with artistic elements can significantly raise the price, but they also create a truly one-of-a-kind look for your property.

3. Material Grade and Thickness

Not all iron fencing is created equal. Thicker pickets and rails mean a stronger, more durable fence, but they also cost more. Commercial-grade wrought iron uses heavier gauge steel and will cost more than lighter residential options. We always recommend choosing a gauge that will stand up to your specific needs, whether that is security, pet containment, or decorative purposes.

4. Terrain and Site Conditions

If your property has slopes, rocky soil, or uneven terrain, installation becomes more complex. Hillside properties in areas like Ventura, Ojai, or Camarillo often require stepped or racked fence panels and custom post placement, which adds to the labor cost.

5. Finish and Coating

A quality finish is essential for protecting your wrought iron fence from rust and corrosion, especially in coastal areas like Ventura County where salt air is a factor. Powder coating is the gold standard and typically costs $5 to $15 per linear foot on top of fabrication costs. Hot-dip galvanizing before powder coating provides the ultimate protection for properties near the coast.

6. Gate Additions

Most fence projects include at least one gate. Single pedestrian gates typically add $500 to $2,000, while double driveway gates can add $2,000 to $8,000+ depending on size and whether they are motorized.

Wrought Iron vs. Aluminum vs. Steel Fencing: Cost Comparison

When shopping for a metal fence, you will encounter three main material options. Here is how they compare:

Wrought Iron Fencing

  • Cost per linear foot (installed): $40 - $200+
  • Pros: Extremely durable, classic timeless look, highly customizable, can be repaired, adds significant property value
  • Cons: Higher upfront cost, requires periodic maintenance to prevent rust, heavier weight
  • Lifespan: 50-100+ years with proper maintenance

Aluminum Fencing

  • Cost per linear foot (installed): $25 - $75
  • Pros: Lightweight, rust-resistant, lower cost, minimal maintenance
  • Cons: Less strong and durable, limited customization, can bend or dent easily, less secure
  • Lifespan: 20-30 years

Steel Fencing

  • Cost per linear foot (installed): $30 - $120
  • Pros: Very strong, good security, more affordable than traditional wrought iron, many style options
  • Cons: Can rust without proper coating, heavier than aluminum, less character than true wrought iron
  • Lifespan: 30-50 years with proper maintenance

For homeowners in Ventura County who want the best combination of durability, beauty, and long-term value, wrought iron remains the top choice. While the upfront investment is higher, the lifespan and aesthetic appeal more than justify the cost over time.

Installation Costs: What to Expect

Professional installation is a significant portion of the total wrought iron fence cost. Here is a breakdown of what installation typically includes:

  • Post hole digging and concrete footings: $15 - $30 per post
  • Panel installation labor: $15 - $40 per linear foot
  • Gate installation: $200 - $800 per gate (labor only)
  • Permits (if required): $50 - $300 in Ventura County

In Ventura County, you may need a building permit for fences over 6 feet tall or for fences in certain zones. Our team at Legendary Metal Works handles the permitting process for you, so you do not have to worry about navigating city or county requirements on your own.

Ventura County Specific Pricing

Living in Ventura County means a few unique cost considerations for your wrought iron fence project:

  • Coastal corrosion protection: Properties in Ventura, Oxnard, and Port Hueneme benefit from upgraded galvanized and powder-coated finishes, adding $5 to $15 per foot but extending fence life dramatically.
  • Fire-resistant fencing: In fire-prone areas of Ojai, Santa Paula, and the hillside communities, wrought iron is an excellent choice because it is completely non-combustible, unlike wood fencing.
  • Local labor rates: Ventura County labor rates are moderate compared to Los Angeles, typically running 10-15% lower for comparable quality work.
  • HOA and neighborhood standards: Many Ventura County communities have specific fence height and style requirements. We are familiar with local HOA standards and can design your fence to comply.

Maintenance Costs Over Time

One important factor in evaluating the true cost of a wrought iron fence is long-term maintenance. Here is what you should budget:

  • Annual inspection and touch-up: $0 - $100 (DIY) or $150 - $300 (professional)
  • Repainting or re-coating (every 5-10 years): $3 - $8 per linear foot
  • Rust repair (as needed): $100 - $500 per section
  • Complete refinishing (every 15-20 years): $10 - $25 per linear foot

With a quality powder-coated finish, your maintenance needs will be minimal for the first decade. Regular inspections and prompt touch-ups of any chips or scratches will keep your fence looking great and prevent costly repairs down the road.

ROI and Property Value Increase

A wrought iron fence is not just an expense; it is an investment. Here is how it pays you back:

  • Property value increase: A quality wrought iron fence can increase your home's value by 5-12%, according to real estate professionals. On a $750,000 Ventura County home, that could mean $37,500 to $90,000 in added value.
  • Curb appeal: Iron fencing is consistently rated as one of the most attractive fencing options by homebuyers and real estate agents.
  • Security premium: Homes with security fencing sell faster and often command higher prices, particularly in suburban and rural areas.
  • Insurance benefits: Some homeowners insurance providers offer discounts for properties with security fencing, particularly if it includes a gated entry.
  • Longevity value: While a wood fence might need full replacement every 15-20 years, a wrought iron fence can last a lifetime, saving you thousands in long-term replacement costs.

How to Get the Best Value on Your Wrought Iron Fence

Here are our top tips for getting the most fence for your budget:

  1. Get multiple quotes: Compare at least 3 estimates, but do not just go with the cheapest. Look at materials, warranty, and reputation.
  2. Choose a local fabricator: Working with a local company like Legendary Metal Works means lower transportation costs, faster turnaround, and a team that understands Ventura County conditions.
  3. Invest in the right finish: Skimping on coating to save a few dollars per foot can cost you thousands in premature rust repair. Powder coating is worth every penny.
  4. Plan your layout carefully: Minimizing corners, angles, and elevation changes can reduce installation costs significantly.
  5. Consider a phased approach: If budget is a concern, you can start with the front of your property for maximum curb appeal and add side and rear fencing later.
